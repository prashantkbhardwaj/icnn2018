About the conference

It is a great pleasure to announce that 5 th International Conference of Nano-materials and Nano-composites (ICNN) will be held at VIT University, Chennai during 08-10 February, 2018.

It is very exciting to see nano-images of materials and their possible immense applications in the daily science column of newspapers. Every month numerous research papers are being published on potential nano-materials and their associated applications for the benefit of the society. Looking at the diversified research findings, it appears that in almost all the fields of materials research, nanostructures are bringing innovation and technological applications. 5 th ICNN, therefore seeks a conglomeration of researchers and experts from academia and industries across the globe, to exchange ideas and intricacies of technological advances in nano-world. Experimental and theoretical aspects on nanoparticles/composites, CNT, graphene and other nanostructures will be addressed in this forum. Special emphasis will be given to interdisciplinary areas of health, energy and environment—their challenges and success stories.

The Previous edition of the conference, Fourth International Conference on Nanostructured Materials and Nanocomposites (ICNM 2017) was held between February 10-12, 2017 at Mahatma Gandhi University, Kottayam, Kerala, India.

We invite you to participate in this conference at VIT University Chennai and exchange ideas and present your findings. It is believed that everyone will be benefited through interaction with experts from various fields leading to enriched research output. Definitely, several research collaborations will be established by the researchers of mutual interest through this conference platform. Awards will be given to outstanding research students for their oral/poster presentations. Special awards will also be announced for the researchers and faculty members for their exceptional research contributions. International and national scientific jurists will decide the selection process.

This conference is being organized in association with International Institute for Carbon Neutral Energy, Kyushu University, Japan. The Industry partner for this conference is Dr. Reddy’s Lab, Hyderabad, India.

What is Nano Technology ?



Nanotechnology ("nanotech") is manipulation of matter on an atomic, molecular, and supramolecular scale. This definition reflects the fact that quantum mechanical effects are important at this quantum-realm scale, and so the definition shifted from a particular technological goal to a research category inclusive of all types of research and technologies that deal with the special properties of matter that occur below the given size threshold. It is therefore common to see the plural form "nanotechnologies" as well as "nanoscale technologies" to refer to the broad range of research and applications whose common trait is size. Because of the variety of potential applications (including industrial), governments have invested billions of dollars in nanotechnology research. Until 2012, through its National Nanotechnology Initiative, the USA has invested 3.7 billion dollars, the European Union has invested 1.2 billion and Japan 750 million dollars.
